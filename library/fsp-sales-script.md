# Foundation Security Program
## Sales Conversation Script

**Goal:** Move from interest to commitment (or clear "not now") in a 20-minute discovery call

**Audience:** Founder/COO of a crypto fund or fintech firm, warm referral from Andrew Hoppin or similar trusted source

---

## Opening (2 min)

"Thanks for taking the time. Andrew mentioned you're thinking about tightening up security. I built Hyla and Amphibian's security program from scratch last year, and I've turned that into a repeatable package. I want to see if it's a fit for where you are right now.

The short version: we implement a full security program in 4 weeks, flat $35K, no surprise hours. The question I'm really asking today is whether that timeline and scope match what you're looking for."

*Pause. Listen.*

---

## Discovery (5–7 min)

Ask in this order:

**1. "Where is security right now?"**
- What tools do you have? (Slack for comms, Google Workspace or M365, any password manager, anything for endpoint protection?)
- Who's managing it? (Founder wearing the hat, or someone dedicated?)
- How many people on the team?

*Listen for:* Current state. Maturity level. Pain points.

**2. "What triggered the conversation? Are you fundraising, or just getting more serious about it?"**

*Listen for:* Urgency and motivation. External pressure (investor asks, partner requirements) vs. internal risk awareness.

**3. "If you could wave a wand, what would be different about security three months from now?"**

*Listen for:* Do they want managed services ongoing, or a one-time buildout? Do they want detection and response, or just "not getting hacked"?

**4. "Have you talked to other security consultants, or is this the first real look?"**

*Listen for:* Price sensitivity, decision-making timeline, what they've heard elsewhere.

---

## Positioning (4–5 min)

Based on their answers, position like this:

**If they have zero tooling:**
"OK so you're starting from scratch. That's actually simpler because there's no tool debt. Here's what I do: I come in, I choose the best-of-breed tools for a 10-person fund (CrowdStrike for EDR, Cloudflare Zero Trust for network, 1Password for passwords, hardened Telegram and Zoom). I configure everything, I push it out, I train your team, and in 4 weeks you have a security program that actually works.

You'll pass investor diligence, your team won't be using weak passwords anymore, and you'll have documented playbooks for bringing people on and off the team. $35K, flat fee, no change orders."

**If they have partial tooling (e.g., Google Workspace + some SaaS):**
"OK so you've got a foundation, but it's not plugged together. Identity's not the real SSO, you've got sprawl, probably some people with standalone passwords, no real device protection. What I do is take what you have—use it as the spine—and fill in the gaps.

Hard wire Google Workspace as your IDP, get everyone on one password manager, add endpoint and network protection, document access to crypto assets, and build playbooks. Four weeks. $35K."

**If they have some tools but want consolidation:**
"You're doing the right things but piecemeal. What I'm offering is the glue—I make all of this work together as a system, instead of isolated tools. That's where the real security is."

---

## Price & Timeline (1–2 min)

Don't over-justify. Just state it.

"$35,000. Four to five weeks. Scope is locked—we don't negotiate scope, we don't do change orders, and the timeline is fixed.

Some people ask if that includes ongoing support after we're done. Answer: No. After 4 weeks, it's your program, you own it. If you want ongoing fractional CISO support, that's a separate conversation—$2,500 a month.

But the first 4 weeks is all-in, everything I mentioned, $35K."

*Pause.*

"Any questions on what's included?"

---

## Closing (2–3 min)

**If they seem interested:**
"Here's how we'd move: You and I agree on a start date, I send over a simple contract that lays out scope, timeline, and pricing. You sign, we kick off, and I start week 1 by getting a clear picture of your current setup and who my point of contact is.

Are you in a place where you could commit to a start date in the next 2–3 weeks?"

*Listen.*

- If yes: "Great. I'm pretty full through [date], but I can probably start [specific week]. Let me check my calendar and send you a contract."
- If unclear timing: "Fair. When do you think you'd realistically be ready to pull the trigger?"
- If they need budget approval: "I get it. What's the timeline for you to get budget squared away? Should we follow up in [2 weeks]?"

**If they seem uncertain or hesitant:**
"Totally fair. This might be the right thing but the wrong time. Or it might be that you want to do this differently—maybe you want to hire a full CISO, or you want ongoing managed services instead of a one-time buildout.

Here's what I'd say: if you get to a place where you want to move fast and you want it done right, you know where to find me. I'm not going anywhere, and Andrew can vouch for the work.

One thing before we wrap: is the $35K the barrier, or is it the timeline, or something else?"

*Listen. Sometimes the real objection is hidden.*

**If they say "this is too expensive":**
"I hear you. For comparison, a fractional CISO doing this work from scratch elsewhere would run $20–30/hr and take 150 hours, so you're looking at $30K in labor alone, plus ongoing retainer. You'd be paying me for the same work, spread over months, and you wouldn't have clear accountability for completion.

This is a fixed price, fixed timeline, fixed scope. You know what you're paying and when it's done.

If that's still not the fit, I totally get it. But I'm not going to discount this because the pricing is based on value, not hours."

**If they say "we want to wait":**
"Totally reasonable. When are you thinking? And what would need to happen between now and then to make this ready to go?"

---

## Bail-Out Conversation (if you're over-time)

"I want to respect your time. Here's the bottom line: Foundation Security Program is $35K, 4 weeks, fixed scope. If that's interesting, I'm ready to move fast. If it's not the fit right now, no worries—reach out when you are.

Any last questions?"

*If no: "Great. I'll send you that one-pager, and let's touch base in a week or two."*

---

## Post-Call (Your Task)

**If they said yes or "let's talk next week":**
- Send a follow-up email within 2 hours with: (1) the Foundation Security Program one-pager, (2) a calendar link to book the kickoff call, (3) simple language about next steps.
- Target: Get them to sign a contract within 1 week.

**If they said no or "wait":**
- Send a brief follow-up: "Enjoyed talking with you. When [X trigger] happens, loop me in."
- Move on. Don't follow up every week—that's needy. Follow up when they've signaled timing.

---

## Tone Reminders

- **Confident, not arrogant.** You've done this for real. You know it works.
- **Direct, not salesy.** Ask what you need to know. State the price without apology.
- **Fast, not rushed.** Respect their time. 20 minutes is your target.
- **Honest, not evasive.** If they ask about limitations, tell them. "This is a foundational program, not enterprise SOC 2. If you need detection and response, that's add-on work."
