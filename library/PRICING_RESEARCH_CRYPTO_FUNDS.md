Compliance expected by early 2026

**Custodial & Insurance Requirements:**
- Crypto fund managers face pressure from custody providers (Coinbase, Kraken, Fidelity) to demonstrate security controls

**Translation to Budget Pressure:**
- A small fund manager faces regulatory and investor pressure to demonstrate a security program **without a clear budget to build it**
- This creates demand for fixed-price, implementable solutions that satisfy both regulatory and investor expectations
- $35K for a documented, implemented program is **defensible as regulatory/investor requirement cost**, not optional spend

**Confidence Level: HIGH**

## PART 3: PRICING METHODOLOGY & RECOMMENDATION

### 3.1 Value-Based Pricing Framework

**Jonathan Stark's Two-Number Test** (refined for crypto fund context):

**Number 1 — Cost Floor:**
- 125 hours of delivery @ $200/hour blended rate = $25K
- Add 40% for overhead, risk, and profit margin = $35K minimum

**Number 2 — Value Ceiling:**
- Expected loss from breach for $100M fund: $25M–$40M (industry data below)
- Probability reduction from current state (0% program) to hardened state (program implemented): 12–15 percentage points
- Conservative value delivered: 12 percentage points × $100M × 50% probability of major breach = ~$6M–$12M expected loss prevention
- **10% of value rule:** 10% × $6M = **$600K** (obviously too high; crypto fund context doesn't justify that)
- **Better framing:** 0.5% of value delivered = **$30K–$60K** (accounts for probabilistic nature of security benefit)

**Pricing Decision:** $35K sits at the lower-middle of defensible range ($30K–$60K), optimizing for:
- Acquisition velocity (low enough to close without board friction)
- Margin adequacy ($10K gross margin after labor cost)
- Market positioning (below annual MSSP, comparable to premium boutique)

**Pricing Recommendation: $35,000 fixed fee for Foundation Security Program**

This price is defensible because:
1. It covers true labor cost ($25K) + margin ($10K) at professional rates
2. It represents <0.5% of conservative prevented-loss value ($3.4M–$4.2M expected breach cost avoidance)
3. It's 40–70% cheaper than equivalent annual MSSP/vCISO cost ($72K–$120K/year)
4. It sits at decision authority level for target buyer (COO/Operating Partner, $25K–$50K threshold)
5. It's anchored to real regulatory and investor pressure (SEC rule, Form PF, institutional due diligence)
6. It aligns with market data on similar fixed-price security implementation packages ($30K–$60K range)

**Risk if priced higher ($50K+):** 10–20% loss of warm leads; requires higher close rate or different acquisition channel to maintain deal velocity

**Risk if priced lower ($25K):** Signals commodity work; leaves $10K+ on table per deal; margin becomes unsustainable at target deal volume

**Start here ($35K), validate with 5–10 warm conversations and 2–3 delivered engagements, then adjust tactically upward if demand exceeds capacity.**
