AI Trust Boundaries

Where data lives, where it flows, and what crosses which boundary. Each ring represents a trust perimeter. Data crossing a ring boundary requires a governance decision.

Ring 1 — Local Sandbox (Green)

Cowork's VM, designated folders, local chat history. Data stays on the user's machine. Lowest risk surface.

Tradeoff: No connectors = 80% of productivity gains left on the table.

Ring 2 — Connector Permissions (Blue)

Each MCP connector grants specific read/write/action capabilities. User authorizes individually. This is the core governance surface — what each connector can see and do.

Key decision: Which connectors, what permissions, does each action require approval?

Ring 3 — Organization Boundary (Yellow)

Google Workspace admin controls, OAuth policies, SaaS discovery tools. The IT layer that governs which connections are even possible for org accounts.

This is where Andy's advisory lives — most firms have zero controls here today.

Ring 4 — AI Provider Infrastructure (Red)

All connector data transits Anthropic's servers for LLM processing. Governed by terms of service, DPA, retention policies, and training opt-out settings.

Consumer Terms (Pro + Team) vs. Commercial Terms (Enterprise) is the material distinction.

The critical insight: Rings 1 and 2 are user-controlled. Ring 3 is admin-controlled. Ring 4 is vendor-controlled. Most executives focus on Ring 4 ("is Anthropic reading my data?") while ignoring Ring 3 ("does my org have any controls on who connects what?"). The advisory engagement starts at Ring 3, because that's where the governance gap is widest and where most firms have done nothing.
