● Data Privacy Mitigations

  • Training opt-out verified in Privacy Settings
  • DPA review before connecting org data
  • Enterprise/commercial terms for sensitive firms
  • Incognito mode for one-off sensitive queries
  • Consumer vs. Commercial terms awareness (Team ≠ Enterprise)

● System Access Mitigations

  • Require approval for all write actions (send, post, create)
  • Designated folder only for file access
  • Per-connector permission review at setup
  • Google Workspace OAuth controls (admin-level)
  • Nudge/Push for ongoing discovery

● Prompt Injection Mitigations

  • Never disable write approval prompts
  • Be skeptical of unsolicited agent actions
  • Anthropic's safety training treats connector data as untrusted
  • Review approval prompts carefully before confirming
  • Category-level risk — not Claude-specific
The pattern: Email is the highest-risk connector across all three categories — highest sensitivity data, most consequential write action (send), and most exposed injection vector (anyone can email you). This is counterintuitive for executives, who think of email as routine. The diagram makes the concentration of risk visible.

Session use: Walk through this column by column for each connector the client plans to enable. The visual heat map immediately shows where the risk concentrates and why certain connectors deserve more governance attention.
