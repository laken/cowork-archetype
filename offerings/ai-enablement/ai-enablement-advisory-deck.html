What This Is

Security-aware AI enablement for executives handling sensitive data. Andy evaluates the client's information risk profile, configures agentic AI tools (starting with Claude Cowork) with appropriate privacy and security controls, and builds working workflows on their actual data. They walk away with a system that's both productive and defensible, plus a written security brief they can hand to their board, investors, or compliance team.
"I'm making sure your adoption of AI doesn't create a liability for your firm. And showing you how powerful it is in the process."
Format: 90-minute initial session (remote or in-person). Assessment + configuration + live demonstration. Follow-on engagement likely for most clients.
Working name: TBD. Candidates: "AI Enablement Advisory," "Executive AI Security Assessment," "AI Workflow & Risk Advisory." Must signal advisory, not tutoring.

Why now, why me

Agentic AI tools are crossing into the mainstream. Claude is the #1 app in the US, Cowork just launched on Windows, CNBC is writing about it for a general business audience. Every executive is hearing about this. The ones who handle sensitive data (fund managers, fintech founders, healthcare, legal) face a specific problem: they can see the productivity gains, but they can't evaluate the information risk.
Their options: (a) dive in and hope for the best, (b) wait for IT to figure it out (which won't happen at a 15-person fund), or (c) find someone who understands both the technology and the risk. Andy is option (c).
The differentiator
20+ years in enterprise IT and security. Direct experience evaluating EDR vendors, data privacy architectures, and access control models. Deep knowledge of Anthropic's specific privacy architecture: consumer vs. commercial terms, connector permission models, data retention policies, prompt injection attack surfaces. Daily Cowork power user who has already mapped the real-world risks. PM/Scrum background that translates technical risk into business language executives understand.

"I brought in an AI security advisor to evaluate our tools and set up our workflows" passes.

"I hired someone to help me set up Claude" doesn't.

Platform validation: Is Cowork the right bet?

Yes, for now. Cowork is the only agentic AI product that combines local file access, MCP connectors (email, calendar, task management), and a sandboxed security model in a consumer-accessible package.
Platform Architecture ICP Fit Risk Level
Claude Cowork Local files + MCP connectors + sandboxed VM Primary Research preview
OpenAI ChatGPT Desktop Conversational + screen sharing, no file/connector architecture Weak No local workflows
Microsoft Copilot Autonomous agents in M365 stack, enterprise IT deployment 12-18mo Not founder self-service yet
Google Workspace Studio Gemini agents in Gmail/Drive/Chat, no local file access Monitor Closest competitor
Key developments: Windows support launched Feb 10, 2026 (platform constraint eliminated). CNBC and VentureBeat coverage positions Cowork for general business users. SaaS stocks dropped 15-33% after enterprise announcements. Adobe, Dentons cited as early adopters.
Strategic framing: The offering is "executive AI enablement." Cowork is the current best vehicle, not the permanent identity. If the platform landscape shifts, the advisory skills and client relationships transfer.
Platform contingency: scenario planning
Privacy incident or outage
Pause sessions, notify active clients with risk assessment, evaluate alternatives. Being first to flag the problem strengthens credibility.
Competitor reaches parity
Add as second supported platform. Advisory value (security evaluation, workflow design, governance) is platform-independent.
Anthropic improves onboarding
Security evaluation and written brief remain valuable. Shift emphasis to "make sure what you're doing is defensible."
Breaking changes on exit from preview
Stay current, update materials weekly. Understanding what changed and what it means for existing configurations is itself advisory value.
Practical guardrails: Monitor Google Workspace Studio, Copilot Tasks, and new entrants (30 min/week). Name and frame the offering platform-agnostically. Build transferable session structure. Security brief includes a platform selection rationale section.

The escalation model

The individual session is a front door, not the whole house. When you configure someone's tools and hand them a security brief, you've demonstrated both technical depth and executive communication in a single interaction. That combination makes the escalation natural.
The security brief creates the opening. A document that says "here's what your current setup exposes, here's what we locked down, and here's what a firm-level assessment would cover" writes the next conversation.
"Can you do this for my team?"
Firm-level AI enablement. Assess org tools, configure per role, establish governance policies, train the team. $10-20K scoped project.
"Our app was built with AI, no one's reviewed it..."
Vibe coder stabilization. Code audit, architecture review, spec-driven development transition. (Elijah Parker archetype.)
"Our investors are asking about AI security..."
The "Show Me the Carfax" play. Security assessment as a due diligence deliverable. Reframes security from cost center to credentialing.
"We want to go AI-first as a company..."
Full AI operations engagement. Strategic roadmap, tool selection, workflow design, change management. $25-50K.
Why the security framing drives scope: A "productivity coaching" session ends when the tools are set up. A "security-aware AI enablement" session surfaces risks that extend beyond the individual: firm data policies, team access controls, vendor evaluation, compliance posture. Those risks don't resolve in 90 minutes. They require follow-on engagement.

Positioning & pricing: the status problem

The concern: A one-off "setup session" risks positioning Andy as a service provider. A 90-minute coaching session, poorly framed, implies "I'll save you time" which is a convenience play, not an authority play. The fix is framing, not format.
1
Lead with security, not productivity. The opening conversation: "Let me evaluate what connecting your email and calendar to an AI agent actually exposes, and configure it so you're protected." Productivity demo happens after the security setup.
2
Deliver a written artifact. Every session ends with a security/privacy brief. Professional deliverable, not a receipt. The document that generates the follow-on conversation.
3
Price as a relationship, not a transaction. See pricing options below.
4
Name it like an advisory service. Avoid "coaching," "setup," "training," "tutorial." The name should make a CEO feel like they're hiring a strategist.

$2,000-2,500
per month, 3-month minimum

scoped assessment
"Personal AI Security & Enablement Assessment." Session + brief + one follow-up. Feels like a project, not a service call.
