Why this holds

What makes this defensible.

The combination is what's rare. Each piece exists elsewhere; the stack doesn't.

04 — Session Structure
How a Tier 1 session runs

The session model.

Pre

Intake survey

Current tools, first 30 mins of workday, what they'd blink and have done. The survey does the intake work so the session starts at workflow, not orientation.

01

Security/architecture walkthrough

10–15 min. Leads the session. What the VM does, what Anthropic sees, what each connector exposes. This is the frame that earns trust and sets up the governance conversation.

02

Workflow mapping

Pick the 1–2 tasks worth automating from the intake. Drudge work vs. judgment work distinction. "What would you blink and have done?" is the right question.

03

Hands-on build – they drive

Client drives. Co-build at least one working thing in the session. This is the critical fix from Ty's feedback.

04

CLAUDE.md setup

If time allows, start the context file. This is the highest-leverage first step and it wasn't completed in Ty session 1 – session 2 priority.

Close

Prioritized next steps

What to automate next, in order. Natural setup for Tier 2 if interest is there.

Lessons from Ty Session 1 (March 10)

  • Security/architecture framing landed well – keep it first
  • Multi-agent debate architecture was the most energizing part – research this seriously (James's 7 agents, Andrew's 8 debating agents)
  • "Drudge work vs. judgment work" framing resonated
  • Demo-heavy approach got "this could be more technical" – session 2 must be hands-on with Ty driving
  • Morning briefing demo registered as "more noise" – trust must be earned before delegation
  • CLAUDE.md not built in session 1 – fix this in session 2
