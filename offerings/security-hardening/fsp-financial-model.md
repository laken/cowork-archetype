## Unit Economics

| Metric | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Price (FSP)** | $35,000 | Fixed, all-in |
| **Delivery hours** | 125 | Solo delivery, 25–30 hrs/week |
| **Weeks to delivery** | 4–5 | Inclusive of discovery, implementation, handoff |
| **Effective hourly rate** | $280/hr | $35K ÷ 125 hrs |
| **Labor cost (fully loaded)** | $25,000 | 125 hrs × $200/hr blended rate |
| **Gross margin** | $10,000 | $35K − $25K |
| **Gross margin %** | 28.6% | Conservative; doesn't include SG&A |

### Why the Labor Cost Estimate?

- **$200/hr blended rate:** This is higher than Andy's previous $150/hr fractional CTO rate, reflecting:
  - Productized delivery (less iteration, more certainty)
  - Higher value per hour (specialized domain expertise)
  - Reduced customer service overhead (fixed scope, not open-ended)

- **125 hours total:** From detailed scope breakdown. Assumes:
  - Cooperative customer (available for testing, training, sign-offs)
  - No significant rework or scope creep
  - Standard tool selection (CrowdStrike, Cloudflare, 1Password—no exotic integrations)
  - One contingency cycle (~10% buffer already included)

This is why productized services + upsells > hourly consulting. You build a recurring customer base.

## Pricing Sensitivity Analysis

### Scenario: What if you raised price to $50K?

| Impact | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Close rate** | Drops from ~30% to ~20% (10% of prospects price-shop) |
| **Win rate in serious segment** | Remains ~90% (price not the decision for ready buyers) |
| **Effective deals/quarter** | Drops from 4 to 3 (1 deal/quarter at lower conversion) |
| **Annual revenue** | $50K × 12 = $600K (vs. $560K at $35K price) |
| **Margin impact** | Gross profit rises to $200K (vs. $160K) |
| **Verdict** | **Net positive** if you're comfortable with lower volume and can fill the capacity with higher-margin work (upsells, ongoing services) |

**Recommendation:** Start at $35K. If you're turning away deals due to capacity constraints and all warm leads are converting (>50% close rate), raise to $45K and absorb the demand drop. Increase price tactically, not speculatively.

## Capacity Planning

### The 2,000-Hour Ceiling

Andy's realistic annual capacity is ~2,000 billable hours (40 hrs/week × 50 weeks, accounting for breaks, admin, sales, proposal writing, etc.).

**How that breaks down:**

**Option 1: FSP-Only Focused**
- 2,000 hours ÷ 125 hours/FSP = **16 FSPs/year**
- Revenue: $560K
- Margin: ~$160K gross
- Problem: Zero capacity for upsells, special projects, or ongoing services

**Option 2: 10 FSPs + Upsells/Ongoing Services (Recommended)**
- 10 FSPs × 125 hours = 1,250 hours
- Remaining 750 hours for:
  - Detection platform implementations (3–5 at $8–12K each)
  - Fractional CISO retainers (2–3 customers @ 40 hrs/month)
  - Annual assessments and ad-hoc work
- Revenue: $350K FSP + $150K upsells/other = **$500K**
- Margin: ~$180K gross (higher average project margin)
- Advantage: Better customer relationships, higher LTV, more flexible capacity planning

**Option 3: Premium / Higher-Touch FSP (~$60K)**
- Fewer deals, higher margin, more capacity for upsells
- 8 FSPs @ $60K = $480K FSP revenue
- Remaining capacity for upsells
- Revenue: ~$500–550K
- Margin: Better, but requires higher conversion rate from warm leads

## Cash Flow Timing

### Important Note on Receivables

FSP is a 4–5 week project. Timing matters for cash flow:

**Proposed payment schedule:**
- 50% upfront at kickoff ($17.5K)
- 50% on completion ($17.5K)

This means:
- Week 0: Receive $17.5K (kickoff)
- Week 5: Receive $17.5K (completion)
- **Total cash lag: 2–3 weeks from start to full receipt**

If you're doing 4 FSPs per quarter on this schedule:
- Q1 start: $70K upfront deposits
- Q1 end / Q2 start: Q1 completion payments roll in
- **Positive cash flow by month 3**

**Alternative: Monthly milestone billing** (if customer prefers)
- 25% at kickoff
- 25% at end of week 2 (IDP + EDR deployed)
- 25% at end of week 4 (full rollout)
- 25% at completion

This spreads cash flow but requires more invoicing overhead.

## Recommendation: Target Operating Model

**Year 1 Goal: 8 FSPs + 2 Upsells**
- Revenue: $280K FSP + $25K upsells = $305K
- Effort: ~1,000 hours FSP + 200 hours upsells = 1,200 billable hours
- Leaves 800 hours for:
  - Sales outreach (50 warm messages/month)
  - Admin, proposals, learning
  - Fractional CISO part-time if opportunities arise
  - Breaks and buffer

- Margin: ~$100K gross, ~$70K net after overhead
- **Outcome:** Prove the model works, generate case studies and testimonials, establish credential in space

**Year 2 Goal: 12–16 FSPs + 5–8 Upsells**
- Revenue: $420–560K FSP + $80–130K upsells = $500–690K
- Effort: ~1,500–2,000 hours FSP + 300–400 hours upsells
- Margin: $150–200K gross, $100–150K net after overhead

**Year 3+:** Operating model is proven. At this point, you either:
1. Raise price ($45–50K per FSP) and maintain 10–12 deals/year
2. Scale with junior consultants delivering FSP under your supervision
3. Shift to higher-margin upsells and fractional CISO retainers
4. All of the above

## Bottom Line

**$35K per FSP is the right entry price because:**

1. It's defensible on value (1,000x ROI for customer)
2. It's competitive vs. fragmented alternatives ($60K+ for comparable work)
3. It generates real profit per deal (~$10K gross margin)
4. It's low enough to close quickly without extended negotiation
5. It leaves room to raise price later as demand proves itself
6. At 8–12 deals/year, it's a sustainable, meaningful business ($300–500K revenue)
7. The real money is in upsells and relationship deepening, not volume of FSP deals

**Risk if you overprice:** You price yourself out of warm referral market and have to prospect cold.
**Risk if you underprice:** You leave $10K+ on the table per deal, or attract price-conscious customers who don't value what you do.

$35K is the Goldilocks price for fintech crypto funds with warm referrals.
